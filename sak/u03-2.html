Unit 3. Part 2

Possession (Grammar)

In Armenian, possession is mainly expressed by

  • possessive determiners or possessive adjectives,
  • possessive articles,
  • possessive pronouns.

Armenian-English Contrasts

Gender

Gender in Armenian grammar is unmarked, i.e. there is no expressed grammatical distinction for masculine, feminine or even neuter gender as is the case in some European languages (cf. German: der, die, das). Therefore, in Armenian gender can be discerned from the linguistic context or situation.

The Armenian personal pronoun in the third person Singular does not express gender as does English with 'he/she/it' and derivations.

The only way to express gender in Armenian is with the nominal suffix -ուհի -uhi, which forms feminine counterparts from masculine nouns indicating persons

աշակերտ

male pupil

աշակերտուհի

female pupil

ուսանող

male student

ուսանողուհի

female student

Gender distinctions are normally conveyed by distinct words

աղջիկ

girl

տղա

boy

հայր

father

մայր

mother

Distinct Third-Person Pronouns

Armenian makes a particular third-person distinction. It has two sets of generic third-person pronouns

  • ինք(ը), նա – for the Singular,
  • իրենք, նրանք – for the Plural.

Singular. These two sets of third-person pronouns prevent ambiguity when two persons are referred to in the same context

  • ինք(ը) – refers to the subject of the sentence or to a word to which prominence is given in a context.
  • նա – hark back to a person who is secondary to the person designated by ինք(ը).

Արամը սիրում է իր ընկերոջը և նրա շանը։

aɾamə siɾum‿e iɾ ənkeɾoʧʰə jev nᵊɾa ʃanə

Aram likes his friend and his dog.

In this example, իր 'his' refers to Aram, while նրա 'his' to Aram’s friend.

Plural. The same distinction is also made in the Plural

  • իրենք 'they' – refers to a number of initially mentioned persons.
  • նրենք 'they' – refers to those that are secondary to the person designated by իրենք 'they'.

Հայերը հարգում են իրենց հյուրերին և պատվում նրանց։

hajeɾə haɾgum‿en iɾenʦʰ hjuɾeɾin jev patvum nᵊɾanʦʰ

Armenians respect their guests and honor them.

In this sentence, իրենց 'their' refers to Armenians, նրանց 'them' to their guests.

Word Formation

Derivation of Abstract Nouns

Nouns in grammar are either concrete (house, man) or abstract (joy, growth). Most abstract nouns are derived from other parts of speech.

The most productive suffixes that build abstract nouns in Armenian are

  • -ություն-utʰjun,
  • -ում-um,
