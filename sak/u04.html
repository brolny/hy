Dialogs

Introducing family and friends

§1

Two friends, A and B, are looking through a family photo album.

Ի՜նչ սիրունիկ երեխա է։

inʧʰ(!) sirunik jereχa‿e

What a lovely baby!

Քրոջս տղան է, վեց ամսական։

kʰᵊroʧʰᵊs tᵊʁan‿e, veʦʰ amsakan

He is my nephew, six months old.

Իսկ ո՞վ է այս համակրելի կինը։

isk ov‿e(?) ajs hamakreli kinə?

And who is this lovely woman?

Եղբորս նշանածն է։

jeʁporᵊs nᵊʃanaʦn‿e

She is my brother’s fiancée.

Իսկ կողքինները ովքե՞ր են։

isk koʁkʰi(n)nerə ovkʰer‿en?

And who are those next to her?

Սա քույրս է, իսկ սա եղբորս տղան է։

sa kʰujrᵊs‿e, isk sa jeʁporᵊs tᵊʁan‿e

This is my sister and this is my nephew.

Ծնողներիդ նկարը ունե՞ս։

ʦᵊnoʁnerit nᵊkarə unes?

Do you have a picture of your parents?

Ահա նրանք՝ նոր նշանված։

aha nᵊrankʰ, nor nᵊʃanvaʦ

Here they are, newly engaged.

Դու մորդ նման ես։

du morᵊt nᵊman‿es

You look like your mother.

Գիտեմ։ Իսկ սա տատիկս է, սա էլ պապիկս՝ մորս ծնողները։

gitem. isk sa tatikᵊs‿e sa el papikᵊs, morᵊs ʦᵊnoʁnerə

I know. And this is my grandmother, and this is my grandfather, my mother’s parents.

Ինչքա՜ն երիտասարդ են։

inʧʰkʰan! jeritasartʰ‿en

How young they are!

Ո՛չ, սա հին նկար է։ Տատս հիմա իննսուն տարեկան է։

voʧʰ(!), sa hin nᵊkar‿e tatᵊs hima innᵊsun tarekan‿e

No, this is an old picture. My grandmother is 90 years old now.

Կարծեմ նոր տեսա նրան այգում։

karʦem nor tesa nᵊran ajgum

I think I just saw her in the garden.

Այո՛, ինքն է, արի՛ ծանոթացնեմ։

ajo(!), inkʰn‿e, ari(!) ʦanotʰaʦʰᵊnem

Yes, it’s her; let me introduce you.

§2

A and B are getting to know each other.

Ծանոթանանք։ Անունս Արամ է։

ʦanotʰanankʰ anunᵊs Aram‿e

Let’s get to know each other. My name is Aram.

ուրախ եմ ծանոթանալու համար։ Մինաս Ազարյան։ Իսկ սա կինս է՝ Կարինեն։

uraχ‿em ʦanotʰanalu hamar minas azarjan isk sa kinᵊs‿e(!), karinen

I am pleased to meet you. Minas Azarian. And this is my wife, Karine.

Շատ ուրախ եմ։ Տանտերը ձեր ի՞նչն է, ձեր եղբա՞յրն է։

ʃat uraχ‿em tanterə ʣer inʧʰn‿e? ʣer jeʁpajrn‿e?

A pleasure to meet you. How is the host related to you? Is he your brother?

Այո՛։ Իսկ դուք եղբորս ընկե՞րն եք։

ajo(!). isk dukʰ jeʁporᵊs ənkern‿ekʰ?

Yes. And are you my brother’s friend?

Ո՛չ, աշխատակիցը։

voʧʰ(!), aʃχatakiʦʰə

No, his colleague.

§3

A is introducing her friend, B, to her mother, C.

Չեմ կարծում, որ իրար ծանոթ ենք։

ʧʰem karʦum, ʋor‿irar ʦanotʰ‿enkʰ

I don’t think we have met.

Մայրի՛կ, սա Արամն է՝ դասընկերս։ Արա՛մ, ծանոթացի՛ր, մայրս է։

majrik, sa aramn‿e(!), dasənkerᵊs aram, ʦanotʰaʦʰir, majrᵊs‿e

Mother, this is Aram, my classmate. Aram, meet my mother.

Արամ։ Շատ ուրախ եմ, տիկին։

aram. ʃat uraχ‿em, tikin

Aram. Very pleased to meet you, Madam.

Ձեր մասին շատ եմ լսել։ Ուրախ եմ, որ վերջապես անձամբ եմ ծանոթանում։

ʣer masin ʃat‿em lᵊsel uraχ‿em ʋor verʧʰapes anʣamb‿em ʦanotʰanum

I have heard a lot about you. I am happy to meet you in person at last.

Նմանապես։

nᵊmanapes

Likewise.

Արա՛մ, արի՛ մյուսներին էլ քեզ ծանոթացնեմ։

aram(!), ari(!) mjusnerin‿el kez ʦanotʰaʦʰənem

Aram, let me introduce you to the others as well.

Սիրո՜վ, գնանք։

sirov!, gᵊnankʰ!

Gladly, let’s go!

The Aorist or the simple past is the only simple Tense in the conjugation paradigm of the Armenian indicative mood. It expresses a completed action in the past without any implication of duration or progression

1. replacing the infinitive endings -ել → -եց, and -ալ → -աց (plus ending -ի)

It is important to note that the Armenian Aorist differs from other past Tenses in that it is the witness’ Tense. It is used only if the speaker has either witnessed the activity or if he/she can speak about it with absolute certainty.

As mentioned before, the Aorist or simple past Tense denotes an action completed at some point in the past without any implication of the duration or progression. In this sense, it differs from the Armenian Imperfect Tense, which indicates a past action in progress

Նկարում էի, երբ դու ներս մտար։

nᵊkarum‿eji, jerpʰ du ners mᵊtar

I was painting when you came in.

The Armenian Aorist or simple past Tense has several equivalents in English translation

  • corresponds to the English simple past,
  • equivalent of the English present perfect.

With reference to usual activities in the past, it corresponds to the English simple past

The nominative case in singular is the citation form of nouns as they appear in dictionaries. It is the initial form in a declension paradigm and has no explicit case markers. It can, however, be used with or without determiners, i.e. a definite article (-ը/-ն) or a possessive article ending in -ս, -դ / -ը, -ն as the case may be.

Nominative case as the subject

The noun in the nominative case serves primarily as the subject of a sentence, a topic about which a comment is made

Անահիտը վառ գույներ էր սիրում։

Anahit liked bright colors.

Գորգս նոր է։

My carpet is new.

In the above sentences, Անահիտը 'Anahit' and գորգս'my carpet' are subjects and appear in the nominative case.

They can be elicited in response to the questions respectively

ո՞վov?who?

Nominative case as subject complement

The secondary syntactic function of the nominative case is also to serve as subject complement, linked to the subject by the auxiliary verb եմ or other copular verbs

Եղբայրս բժիշկ է։

jeʁpajrᵊs bᵊʒiʃk‿e

My brother is a physician.

Արամը մնում է ընկերս։

Aram remains my friend.

In the first sentence, both the subject եղբայրս'my brother' and its complement բժիշկ'physician' appear in the nominative case.

They are linked to each other by the copula է'is'. In the second sentence, it is the verb մնալ'to remain' that links the subject Արամը 'Aram' to ընկերս'my friend' and both nouns are used in the nominative case.
